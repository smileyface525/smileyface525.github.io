Dealing with Conflict

April 5, 2014

  1. Discuss a time you had an emotional conflict with someone. What was it about? What happened?

    Recently I had an argument with my co-worker Jeff. He had been making small mistakes for a while and my boss, who doesn't usually work with him asked me to have a duscussion with Jeff about how to fix the problem. So I talked to him the next day and asked him what he thinks is the best way to eliminate mistakes. He was cooperative for a lille while but then he started pointing out other people's mistakes and got upset. That's when we had the argument.

  2. What was the source of the conflict?

    Basiaccly Jeff thoguht that the boss should have had a discussion with him in person instead of me doing it for her. So he started venting all of his complaints and emotions out to me.

  3. How did you handle that conflict?

    In the beginning I was trying to be calm and to just listen to him. And even though I did not feel comfortable, I was doing a decent job listening until he started pointing my mistakes. Then I ended up yelling at him once.

  4. Did your actions make the conflict better or worse?

    I'm not sure if it did anything. I guess it worked in a way because he stopped complaing and yelling.

  5. If you could go back, what would you do differently, if anything?

    I would not yell and I should have just listened to him and told the boss to talk to him later.

  6. What did you learn from this experience?

    When someone is angry, let them vent and don't take it personally. This conflict was a good lesson for me because when I'm with angry people, I tend to get intimidated and get angry myself, which is not a good pattern!
